3D Modeling Techniques

Polygon Modeling

Creating 3D objects using polygonal meshes, the most common technique for hard surface modeling and basic garment structure creation.

Beginner

Digital Sculpting

Organic modeling technique using virtual clay-like tools to create detailed, high-resolution fashion accessories and intricate surface details.

Intermediate

NURBS Modeling

Precision modeling using mathematical curves and surfaces, ideal for creating smooth, exact shapes in luxury accessories and jewelry.

Advanced

Cloth Simulation

Physics-based simulation of fabric behavior, including draping, folding, and interaction with body forms and environmental forces.

Expert

UV Mapping

Process of projecting 3D model surfaces onto 2D texture space, essential for applying realistic fabric patterns and surface details.

Intermediate

3D Modeling Workflow

Concept & Reference

Gather design references, sketches, and technical drawings. Define the project scope, style requirements, and intended use of the 3D model.

1

Base Mesh Creation

Create the basic 3D form using appropriate modeling techniques. Establish proper topology and proportions for the garment or accessory.

2

Detail Development

Add design details, seam lines, pockets, buttons, and other fashion elements. Refine the silhouette and ensure accuracy to the original design.

3

UV Mapping & Texturing

Unwrap the 3D model for texture application. Create or apply fabric textures, patterns, and surface materials for realistic appearance.

4

Simulation & Fitting

Apply cloth simulation for natural draping and fit testing. Adjust simulation parameters to match real fabric behavior and desired aesthetic.

5

Lighting & Rendering

Set up professional lighting schemes and camera angles. Render high-quality images for presentations, catalogs, or marketing materials.

6

Post-Processing & Delivery

Enhance rendered images with color correction and compositing. Prepare final deliverables in required formats for intended use.

7
