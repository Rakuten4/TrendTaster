Understanding the Color Wheel

The color wheel is the foundation of color theory, showing the relationships between colors and how they interact with each other. It consists of primary, secondary, and tertiary colors arranged in a circular format.

Primary colors (red, blue, yellow) cannot be created by mixing other colors. Secondary colors (orange, green, purple) are created by mixing two primary colors. Tertiary colors are formed by mixing a primary and secondary color.

Hue

The pure color itself - red, blue, green, etc.

Saturation

The intensity or purity of the color

Value

The lightness or darkness of a color

Color Schemes

Monochromatic

Uses different shades, tints, and tones of a single color. Creates harmony and sophistication in fashion design.

Analogous

Uses colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. Creates pleasing and comfortable designs.

Complementary

Uses colors opposite each other on the color wheel. Creates vibrant contrast and visual impact.

Triadic

Uses three colors equally spaced on the color wheel. Offers strong visual contrast while maintaining harmony.

Color Psychology in Fashion

Red

Symbolizes passion, energy, and confidence. Often used in power dressing and statement pieces to command attention.

Passion Energy Confidence

Blue

Represents trust, calmness, and professionalism. Popular in business attire and creates a sense of reliability.

Trust Calm Professional

Green

Associated with nature, growth, and harmony. Often used in sustainable fashion and creates a refreshing feel.

Nature Growth Harmony

Orange

Conveys enthusiasm, creativity, and warmth. Perfect for casual wear and creates an approachable, friendly vibe.

Enthusiasm Creativity Warmth

Purple

Symbolizes luxury, creativity, and mystique. Often used in high-end fashion to convey sophistication.

Luxury Creativity Mystique

Black

Represents elegance, power, and timelessness. The ultimate color for formal wear and creates dramatic impact.

Elegance Power Timeless

Color Applications in Fashion

Seasonal Collections

Use warm colors for fall/winter collections and cool colors for spring/summer to align with seasonal moods.

Professional Wear

Neutral and cool colors like navy, gray, and black convey professionalism and trustworthiness in business attire.

Evening Wear

Rich, deep colors and metallics create elegance and sophistication for formal and evening occasions.

Activewear

Bright, energetic colors motivate and inspire activity while cool colors provide a calming effect during workouts.
